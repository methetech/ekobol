How Revenue Sharing Works at Ekobol

By The Ekobol Finance Team on July 20, 2025

Ekobol embraces a revenue-sharing model rather than traditional subscription fees for some of its plans, specifically the Basic Plan and Partner Plan. This model means your success is directly tied to ours, fostering a collaborative and transparent relationship. Here's how revenue sharing works at Ekobol:

Revenue Sharing Fundamentals

In our revenue-sharing model, instead of charging you a fixed monthly fee, we take a percentage of the profit you generate from your sales for specific plans. This means you only pay us when you succeed.

1. Basic Plan Revenue Share (10%)

  • Model: This plan gives you full access to our AI tools and all platform features. You retain control and manage most of your operations yourself.
  • Calculation: We take 10% of the profit you make from each successful sale. This profit is calculated after deducting product costs and marketplace commissions.
  • Billing: Typically calculated monthly and automatically deducted from your earnings or billed to a designated payment method.

2. Partner Plan Revenue Share (50%)

  • Model: This is our full-service plan where Ekobol builds and manages your digital empire on your behalf. You focus on creating and shipping products, while we handle everything from marketing to operations.
  • Calculation: We take 50% of the profit you make from each successful sale. The higher percentage compensates for us fully taking on your digital operational burden.
  • Billing: Automatically deducted from your earnings or billed monthly via your designated payment method.

3. Dynamic Plan (Custom Share)

  • Model: This plan offers a custom revenue share that adjusts based on how the workload is split between you and our platform. The more tasks you take on, the higher your share of the revenue.
  • Calculation: The percentage is custom-determined based on the scope of services and operations Ekobol's team undertakes. This is a personalized agreement.
  • Billing: Calculated and billed monthly as per the agreement.

How is "Profit" Defined?

The definition of "profit" may vary slightly depending on your specific plan, but generally it includes:

  • Gross Sales Revenue: The selling price of the product.
  • Minus Product Costs: The cost incurred to produce or source the product.
  • Minus Marketplace Fees/Commissions: Fees taken by marketplaces like Trendyol, Etsy, Amazon, etc.
  • Minus Shipping Costs: Costs associated with getting the product to the customer (if applicable and included in the agreement).

The net profit is the amount remaining after these deductions. Ekobol then takes the agreed-upon percentage of this net profit.

Billing and Transparency

  • Monthly Statements: At the end of each billing cycle, you will be provided with a detailed statement. This statement will clearly outline all your sales, deductions, and Ekobol's share.
  • Dashboard Tracking: You can monitor real-time data regarding your revenue, expenses, and net profit from your finance dashboard.

Our revenue-sharing model reinforces our commitment to your business's growth. The more you earn, the more we earn, aligning us towards shared objectives.
